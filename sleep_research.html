Survey on Automatic Sleep Stage Classification

Automated sleep stage classification using polysomnography (PSG) signals, especially EEG, EOG, EMG, and ECG, has advanced significantly in recent years. This survey summarizes state-of-the-art methods including CNNs, Transformers, Attention Mechanisms, and multi-modal learning.

Datasets

  • Sleep-EDF Expanded: 197 recordings, EEG/EOG/EMG, R&K Link
  • SHHS: 9736 recordings, EEG/EOG/EMG/ECG/airflow, R&K/AASM Link
  • MASS: 200 recordings, EEG/EOG/EMG, AASM Link
  • ISRUC: 100+ recordings, EEG/EOG/EMG
  • CAP Sleep Database: 108 recordings, EEG/EOG/EMG/Respiration Link

Model Architectures

  • CNNs: SleePyCo, DeepSleepNet-Lite, Wavelet-based CNN
  • RNN / LSTM: L-SeqSleepNet, RED (BLSTM + CNN)
  • Transformers: Cross-Modal Transformers, SleepEGAN, FPJA-Net
  • Hybrid / Multi-Modal: NeuroNet, MGANet, BIOT

Evaluation Metrics

  • Accuracy (%)
  • Macro F1-score
  • Cohen's kappa (Îº)
  • Sensitivity & Precision per stage
  • Dataset-specific examples: ISRUC-SG1 Accuracy 97.74%, F1 93.75%; Sleep-EDF Accuracy ~96%

Challenges & Future Directions

  • Class imbalance (especially N1 stage) remains a challenge.
  • Generalization across populations and sleep disorders is limited.
  • Wearable EEG devices require adaptation to frontal channels.
  • Multi-modal fusion improves accuracy but increases computational complexity.
  • Lightweight models like FPJA-Net and CausalAttenNet enable real-time applications.
